The right ventricle is a vital chamber of the heart, responsible for pumping deoxygenated blood to the lungs for oxygenation. It works as part of the pulmonary circulation system and is essential for maintaining the flow of blood through the heart and lungs.

Pumping to the Lungs:

  • The right ventricle pumps the deoxygenated blood into the pulmonary artery, which leads to the lungs. In the lungs, the blood picks up oxygen and releases carbon dioxide, completing the pulmonary circuit.

Summary of Blood Flow through the Right Ventricle:

  1. Step 1: Blood enters the right ventricle from the right atrium via the tricuspid valve.

  3. Step 3: Blood flows into the pulmonary artery and heads to the lungs for oxygenation.

Importance:

The right ventricle plays a critical role in maintaining pulmonary circulation by ensuring that deoxygenated blood reaches the lungs to become oxygenated. Its proper functioning is vital for oxygenating the blood and sustaining life.
