Human Heart

What is Human Heart?

The human heart is a muscular organ, about the size of a fist, that plays a crucial role in maintaining life. It pumps blood throughout the body, delivering oxygen and nutrients to tissues and removing carbon dioxide and other waste products. The heart has four chambers: two upper chambers called atria and two lower chambers called ventricles. These chambers work in a synchronized manner, with the atria receiving blood and the ventricles pumping it out to the lungs and the rest of the body.

The heart also has valves that ensure blood flows in the right direction. It operates through an electrical system that controls the heart's rhythm, enabling it to beat in a steady, coordinated way. Blood circulation is divided into two pathways: pulmonary circulation (blood to and from the lungs) and systemic circulation (blood to the rest of the body).

In essence, the heart is like a living engine that fuels our body by maintaining constant blood flow, ensuring we can function properly.
